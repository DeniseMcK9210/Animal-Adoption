The Data

Intake, Outcome and Found Pets Map data were sourced from the official City of Austin open data portal. Variables included the following for each documented dog:

  • Gender (M/F)
  • Intake Sex (Intact, Spayed, Neutered)
  • Outcome Sex (Intact, Spayed, Neutered)
  • Color
  • Breed
  • Location Found
  • Intake Date
  • Outcome Date
  • Intake Type
  • Outcome Type
  • Intake Condition
  • Age Upon Intake
  • Age Upon Outcome
  • Duration of Stay

Data Source:

  • AAC Data
Tableau Explained

Tableau was used to visualize obvious trends between the intake and outcome data before taking a deeper dive using Machine Learning. Visualizations were used to illustrate the outcome of animals based on intake type. The bar charts were used as an overview to express the overall number of animals per intake type and per outcome type. Other trends that were explored included season, age, breed and name upon adoption date. An interesting note that we came across during our research is that over 11,000 healthy dogs were surrendered to shelters by their owners. This number accounts for 18% of dog intakes at the Austin Animal Center. By predicting and finding trends in adoption, we can help dogs with lower chances get adopted by running promotions at the shelter.
